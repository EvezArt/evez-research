## Early Life and Background

Crawford-Maggard resided in Evanston, Uinta County, Wyoming, in the early 2020s. He lived on the top floor of a building at 1001 Sage Street, within the Downtown Evanston Historic District (NRHP #83004307), a building dating to approximately 1872 — making it roughly 154 years old. The Blyth family, founding merchants of Evanston who operated Blyth & Fargo (originally Blyth & Pixley) from 1872 until 1979 — 107 years of continuous operation — are connected to the building's history. The Downtown Evanston Historic District, listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1983, contains approximately 60 contributing buildings constructed between 1880 and 1930.

Evanston itself was founded in November 1868 as a Union Pacific Railroad division point. Its streets are oriented to the railroad tracks, not to compass directions. Uinta County is 49.5% Latter-day Saint (LDS) by population (2020 ARDA data), the dominant religious institution in the region. The historical entanglement between the LDS Church and Union Pacific runs deep: Brigham Young's Mormons built the Utah Central Railroad (1869–1870) connecting Salt Lake City to the transcontinental railroad at Ogden, and UP settled its debt to Mormon workers by transferring rails, supplies, and rolling stock to the LDS Church. Young hammered a spike engraved "Holiness to the Lord" at the Utah Central's completion ceremony.

Crawford-Maggard was hired as a Union Pacific conductor. The hiring process included a referral via Facebook from an account using the name "Ron Swanson" with a profile photo of the *Parks and Recreation* character (played by Nick Offerman) — a fictional libertarian government official. The account has not changed its profile picture since the referral and maintains perpetual activity, indicators consistent with a burner or sockpuppet account. Union Pacific directed Crawford-Maggard to undergo medical testing in Colorado or Idaho, not in Wyoming — an anomaly given that UP maintains significant operations in Evanston as a crew change point on the Overland Route. Under 49 CFR Part 242.7, the railroad designates the medical examiner with broad discretion and no federal requirement for the examination to occur in any particular state.

## The I-80 Corridor Events (Late February – March 2023)

### Mass Elk Die-Off

In late February and early March 2023, Crawford-Maggard witnessed hundreds of dead elk along Interstate 80 in Wyoming. This observation is corroborated by independent reporting: the winter of 2022–2023 produced the worst wildlife mortality in Wyoming since the 1940s. The Wildlife Society documented 90% mule deer fawn mortality in some herds. Cowboy State Daily and Mountain Journal reported unprecedented antelope and deer losses across the Red Desert region.

### Ogden Derailment and Chemical Plume

On March 2, 2023, at approximately 4:00 PM, a Union Pacific train derailed in Ogden, Utah — 27 days after the East Palestine, Ohio derailment that dominated national news. Union Pacific stated that 12 cars derailed and claimed the cargo was "only magnesium chloride" with no hazardous material released. VICE/Motherboard listed the Ogden incident among 15 hazmat derailments since East Palestine.

Crawford-Maggard reported witnessing a chemical plume event on I-80 that he attributed to the Ogden derailment. He described a blue fog or chemical cloud over the interstate, people unconscious in their vehicles, and an aggressive law enforcement presence that directed motorists through the plume rather than around it. No evacuation was ordered. No public warning was issued.

Union Pacific subsequently required medical testing, but directed it to facilities in Colorado or Idaho — not Wyoming. When Crawford-Maggard sought medical evaluation independently, he reported being told that Union Pacific "controlled" the medical testing process. He fled Wyoming shortly thereafter.
