**Source:** commerce.senate.gov (Senate Commerce Committee press release)

- FRA "had no choice but to end data collection activities"

## 4. Documentation of Systematic Absence of News Coverage (Spectral Evidence)

### What "Spectral Evidence" Means
In this context, spectral evidence refers to the pattern of absence across multiple independent information systems. When an event that should leave documentary traces (news articles, NTSB investigations, FRA reports, local news coverage) leaves NO traces across ANY system, the absence itself becomes evidence — either of effective suppression or of an event being deliberately kept below public reporting thresholds.

### Search Systems Consulted (All Returned No Results for Ogden March 2023)
1. **Bing Search** — 14+ separate queries with varied terms
2. **Google Search** — blocked by anti-bot measures but 5+ queries attempted
3. **DuckDuckGo** — blocked by CAPTCHA
4. **web_search API** — 7 queries, 1 partial result (NTSB general)
5. **NTSB.gov direct investigation pages** — 10 pages checked, 0 for Ogden
6. **Wikipedia** — "2023 in rail transport" and "List of US rail accidents" — no Utah March 2023 event
7. **KSL.com** (Utah's primary news site) — search returned empty
8. **Deseret News** (major Utah newspaper) — search returned empty
9. **Standard-Examiner** (Ogden's local newspaper) — 404 error on search
10. **Wayback Machine CDX API** — all 3 queries failed/timeed out

### The Pattern of Absence

**What should exist if a March 2, 2023 derailment occurred in Ogden, UT:**
- A local news article in the Standard-Examiner (Ogden's paper)
- A KSL.com or Deseret News report
- An NTSB preliminary report (if significant enough)
- An FRA accident report entry
- A PHMSA hazmat incident report (if chemicals were involved)
- Possible national news pickup (given post-East Palestine sensitivity)
- Union Pacific press release or statement

**What was found: NONE OF THESE.**

### Alternative Explanations for the Absence

1. **The event was too minor to report:** A small derailment without hazmat release might not generate news. But Steven Crawford-Maggard witnessed a chemical plume, suggesting hazmat involvement.

2. **The event was actively suppressed:** Given UP's documented pattern of information control (FRA audit interference, employee coaching), suppression is plausible. UP has the resources and demonstrated willingness to manage information flow.

3. **The date is slightly off:** The event may have occurred on a nearby date (late February or early March 2023). NTSB RRD23LR006 is March 4, 2023 in Springfield, OH — showing events were being recorded in this timeframe.

4. **The event was classified as minor by FRA:** FRA has reporting thresholds ($10,500 damage). If the event was below threshold, it might not appear in federal databases but would still appear in local news.

5. **The event occurred but was handled quietly:** Ogden is a major UP rail hub. Local media might not have been alerted if UP quickly contained the scene.

6. **The chemical plume was from a different source:** The plume on I-80 could have originated from a non-rail source (truck spill, industrial accident) that was attributed to a train derailment by the witness.
