### NATURE OF COMPLAINT

This complaint alleges a breach of the federal trust obligation to the Morongo Band of Mission Indians through the failure of the Department of the Interior and the Bureau of Reclamation to protect tribal water quality from documented industrial contamination of the Colorado River system.

### FACTUAL BASIS

1. The Morongo Band of Mission Indians (36,000-40,000 acres, Cabazon, California) hold tribal trust land in the Coachella Valley.

2. The Coachella Valley groundwater is contaminated with perchlorate, chromium-6, arsenic, uranium, nitrates, and other contaminants.

3. A major source of perchlorate is the Kerr McGee Chemical facility in Henderson, Nevada (now Occidental Petroleum via Anadarko acquisition). Kerr McGee contaminated the Colorado River with perchlorate. The Colorado River water is used for Coachella Valley irrigation and aquifer recharge.

4. The Bureau of Reclamation (an agency within the DOI) operates Davis Dam, which controls the Colorado River water that carries the contamination from Henderson, NV to the Coachella Valley.

5. The EPA has NOT designated a Superfund site for the Coachella Valley.

6. The Navajo Nation received $5.15 billion in the Anadarko/Kerr McGee settlement (2014) for DIRECT contamination (uranium mines on tribal land). The Morongo Band has received NOTHING for INDIRECT contamination (perchlorate via Colorado River).

7. The DOI has a documented revolving door with the fossil fuel industry: at least 9 officials moved between DOI and oil/gas/coal lobbying positions (J. Steven Griles, Gale Norton, David Bernhardt, William Perry Pendley, Scott Angelle, Doug Domenech, James Cason, Douglas Matheney, Steven Winberg).

8. Roger Stone lobbied for Uinta Basin Railway Holdings on "Railroad construction in Utah" — oil-by-rail expansion through the same corridor where the Ogden, UT derailment occurred.

### LEGAL BASIS

The Federal Trust Doctrine imposes a fiduciary duty on the United States to protect tribal resources, including water quality on tribal trust land. The DOI's failure to act — despite documented contamination, despite operating the infrastructure (Davis Dam) that transports the contamination, despite the EPA's refusal to designate a Superfund site — constitutes a breach of that fiduciary duty.

The revolving door between the DOI and the fossil fuel industry creates a structural conflict of interest that prevents the DOI from fulfilling its trust obligation.

### RELIEF REQUESTED

1. OIG investigation of the DOI's failure to protect Morongo Band water quality
2. Review of the revolving door between DOI and the fossil fuel industry
3. Audit of Bureau of Reclamation's knowledge of Colorado River contamination and its failure to act
4. Comparison of the Navajo Nation settlement ($5.15B) with the Morongo Band's lack of compensation
5. Investigation of Roger Stone's lobbying for Uinta Basin Railway and any DOI involvement
