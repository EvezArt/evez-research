### NATURE OF COMPLAINT

This complaint alleges a pattern of systemic civil rights violations by federal agencies through deliberate regulatory failure resulting in disproportionate environmental harm to tribal communities and historically marginalized populations across multiple states, in violation of:

- Title VI of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 (42 U.S.C. Section 2000d) — discrimination in federally funded programs
- The Safe Drinking Water Act (42 U.S.C. Section 300f et seq.) — failure to ensure safe drinking water
- The Federal Trust Doctrine — breach of fiduciary duty to tribal nations
- The Due Process Clause of the Fifth Amendment — deprivation of life, liberty, and property without due process

### STATEMENT OF FACTS

**I. Rail Derailment Pattern (2021-2024)**

Union Pacific Railroad, operating under Precision Scheduled Railroading (a cost-cutting operating model pushed by Soroban Capital Management, which held a $1.6 billion stake), derailed in at least 8 incidents across 6 states in 3 years:

1. East Palestine, OH (2/3/2023) — Norfolk Southern, 38 cars, vinyl chloride controlled burn (OxyVinyls chemical)
2. Ogden, UT (3/2/2023) — Union Pacific
3. Cheyenne, WY (3/13/2023) — Union Pacific
4. Albert Lea, MN (5/15/2021) — Union Pacific, 28 cars, 40,000 gallons HCl into wetlands near Goose Lake
5. Sibley, IA (5/2021) — Union Pacific, hazmat evacuation
6. Mason City, IA (4/2024) — Union Pacific
7. Raymond, MN (3/30/2023) — BNSF, 22 cars, ethanol fire
8. McDavid, FL (7/2021) — CSX, ammonium nitrate + molten sulfur

**II. Colorado River Contamination Chain**

Kerr McGee Chemical (now Occidental Petroleum via Anadarko acquisition) contaminated the Colorado River with perchlorate from its Henderson, Nevada facility. The perchlorate traveled downstream to communities including:
- Laughlin, Nevada (drinks Colorado River water, no federal MCL for perchlorate)
- Bullhead City, Arizona (groundwater chromium-6 at 1.39 ppb, 70x EWG health guideline)
- Coachella Valley, California (groundwater contaminated with perchlorate, chromium-6, arsenic, uranium, nitrates)
- Morongo Band of Mission Indians (tribal trust land in contaminated zone, no Superfund designation, no settlement)

Separately, PG&E's Topock Compressor Station discharged chromium-6 (1951-1964), creating a groundwater plume migrating toward the Colorado River. The contamination was contained but NOT settled, unlike the parallel Hinkley, CA contamination which resulted in a $333M settlement.

**III. Tribal Trust Breach**

The Morongo Band of Mission Indians (36,000-40,000 acres, Cabazon, CA) has tribal trust land in the Coachella Valley. The federal government has a trust obligation to protect tribal water quality. The EPA has NOT designated a Superfund site for the Coachella Valley. The Morongo Band has received NO compensation for the contamination. The Navajo Nation received $5.15 billion for DIRECT contamination (Kerr McGee uranium mines). The Morongo Band has received NOTHING for INDIRECT contamination (perchlorate via Colorado River). The direct/indirect distinction is the legal architecture that denies the claim.

**IV. Database Erasure**

Three federal databases that track derailments and hazardous materials incidents are non-functional or have had data removed:
- FRA safety database
- NTSB database
- PHMSA database

**V. Lobbying to Weaken Safety Law**

The Association of American Railroads ($4.4M+ in 2023) and Occidental Petroleum ($5.9M in 2023) spent a combined $10.3M+ to weaken the Railway Safety Act of 2023, which was introduced BECAUSE of the East Palestine derailment. The manufacturer of the chemical that was on the derailed train lobbied to weaken the law written because of the disaster.

**VI. Revolving Door**

The Department of the Interior (parent agency of the Bureau of Reclamation, which controls the Colorado River water carrying the contamination) has a documented revolving door with the fossil fuel industry: at least 9 officials moved between DOI and oil/gas/coal lobbying (J. Steven Griles, Gale Norton, David Bernhardt, William Perry Pendley, Scott Angelle, Doug Domenech, James Cason, Douglas Matheney, Steven Winberg).

### LEGAL CLAIMS

1. The FRA violated the civil rights of communities along rail corridors by failing to regulate the transport of hazardous materials through populated areas, disproportionately affecting low-income and minority communities.

2. The EPA violated the civil rights of tribal communities by failing to enforce environmental laws on tribal trust land and by failing to designate a Superfund site for documented contamination.

3. The DOI breached its federal trust obligation to the Morongo Band of Mission Indians by failing to protect tribal water quality from Colorado River contamination originating from a facility regulated by the federal government.

4. The FRA, EPA, and PHMSA violated due process by maintaining non-functional databases that prevent public access to safety information.

5. The combined regulatory failure constitutes a pattern of deliberate indifference to the health and safety of communities along documented contamination corridors.

### RELIE REQUESTED

1. Investigation of the documented pattern of regulatory failure across FRA, EPA, PHMSA, and DOI
2. Designation of a Superfund site for the Coachella Valley groundwater contamination
3. Establishment of a federal Maximum Contaminant Level for perchlorate
4. Restoration of all removed or unavailable data from the FRA, NTSB, and PHMSA databases
5. Referral of the tribal compensation asymmetry (Navajo $5.15B vs Morongo $0) for investigation by the Civil Rights Division
6. Investigation of the $10.3M lobbying expenditure by entities that caused the East Palestine disaster to weaken the Railway Safety Act
