## 1. ELK DIE-OFF (Horse Creek Feedground, Winter 2022-2023)

**Source:** WyoFile — "Historic die-off at Wyoming elk feedground prompts management changes"
**Author:** Mike Koshmrl
**URL:** https://wyofile.com/historic-die-off-at-wyoming-elk-feedground-prompts-management-changes/
**Date:** Published 2023
**Key facts confirmed:**
- 155 elk calves died or euthanized on Horse Creek Feedground
- 64 additional dead calves found on Bridger-Teton National Forest / private land
- 45% calf mortality rate
- First reports: Feb 25, 2023 (lethargic calves spotted by feeder)
- Peak: 5-10 animals per day succumbing
- Largest documented hoof rot die-off in ~100 years of Wyoming elk feeding
- Game and Fish did NOT publicize the die-off (reported only in "seldom-read" Job Completion Report)
- Biologist: Ben Wise (WGFD disease biologist, Jackson Region)
- Quote: "What's managing the population now is calf mortality"
- CWD also detected at Horse Creek Feedground
