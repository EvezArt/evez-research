# OSINT Investigation: Wyoming Brain Injury Rehabilitation Facilities & Healthcare Network for TBI Patients

**Date of Investigation:** 2026-06-28
**Investigator:** OSINT Subagent
**Classification:** Open Source Intelligence

---

## Executive Summary

Wyoming has extremely limited dedicated brain injury rehabilitation infrastructure. The state has no freestanding TBI rehabilitation hospital. Only one facility — Cheyenne Regional Medical Center's Acute Rehabilitation Unit — explicitly lists traumatic brain injury as a treated condition. Wyoming relies heavily on out-of-state facilities, particularly Craig Hospital in Englewood, Colorado, for serious TBI rehabilitation. The Wyoming Department of Health has no visible dedicated TBI program or brain injury waiver. The Wyoming Department of Corrections has been the subject of lawsuits alleging unconstitutional conditions, and the state has never had a DOJ pattern-or-practice investigation of its law enforcement agencies.

## 1. BRAIN INJURY REHABILITATION FACILITIES IN WYOMING

### 1A. Cheyenne Regional Medical Center (CRMC) — Cheyenne, WY
**STATUS: PRIMARY TBI REHAB FACILITY IN WYOMING**

- **Acute Rehabilitation Unit (ARU):** 19-bed (15 private rooms) accredited inpatient rehabilitation unit
- **Explicitly treats:** Traumatic brain injuries, stroke, spinal cord injuries, multiple trauma, neurological conditions, amputation, orthopedic conditions
- **Therapies offered:** Physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech/language therapy, cardiac rehab, cardiopulmonary services, LSVT Loud/Big (neurological programs)
- **Admissions criteria:** Requires physician specializing in physical rehabilitation, minimum 2 types of therapy (PT/OT/ST), 24-hour rehabilitation nursing, ability to tolerate 3 hours of therapy/day, 5 of 7 days/week
- **Contact:** (307) 633-7310
- **Address:** 214 E. 23rd St., Cheyenne, WY (West Campus); 2600 E. 18th St. (East Campus)
- **Website:** https://www.cheyenneregional.org/service/acute-rehabilitation-unit/

**Neurosurgery Program at CRMC:**
- Treats traumatic brain injury, aneurysms, cerebral vascular disease, minimally invasive spine surgery
- Uses Stealth Treon system (3D surgical navigation), MRI, ultra-fast CT scanners, 3D/4D ultrasound
- First U.S. medical center to use Stealth Treon for brain tumor removal (2003)

**Trauma Services at CRMC:**
- **Level III Trauma Center** (verified by American College of Surgeons)
- Highest state trauma designation
- Neurosurgery capabilities
- In-house helicopter service with rooftop helipad
- Trauma surgeons trained in Advanced Trauma Life Support
- Board-certified physicians

## 2. OUT-STATE TBI REHABILITATION (ROCKY MOUNTAIN REGION)

### 2A. Craig Hospital — Englewood, Colorado
**STATUS: PREMIER ROCKY MOUNTAIN TBI REHAB — ACCEPTS WYOMING PATIENTS**

- **Location:** 3425 S. Clarkson Street, Englewood, Colorado (just south of Denver)
- **Specialty:** Spinal cord injury (SCI) and traumatic brain injury (TBI) rehabilitation and research
- **Type:** 93-bed, private, not-for-profit, free-standing long-term acute care and rehabilitation hospital
- **Founded:** 1907 (originally for tuberculosis treatment, evolved into rehab)
- **National Designations:**
  - NIDRR Model System Center for BOTH SCI and TBI
  - NIDRR National Statistical TBI Database (manages data for all 16 TBI Model System Centers in U.S.)
  - TBI Model System designation since 1998
  - SCI Model System grant since 1974
- **Patient Volume:** ~55 SCI inpatients, ~30 TBI inpatients, ~50-60 outpatients at any given time
- **Out-of-State Patients:** Half of Craig's patients come from outside Colorado; has treated patients from all 50 states and foreign countries in recent years
- **Family Housing:** Provides housing for out-of-state families and outpatients, first 30 days free for families of new inpatients
- **Research:** 23-person research department, $4 million annual budget (federal, state, foundation, industry grants)
- **Funding Sources:** NIDRR, CDC, Colorado TBI Trust Fund, CDMRP (Dept. of Defense), pharmaceutical/medical equipment companies
- **Campus:** $90 million expansion completed 2016 (85,000 sq ft new, 135,000 sq ft renovated)
- **Notable Patients:** Columbine massacre victims, Roy Horn (Siegfried & Roy), Kevin Pearce (snowboarder), Steven McDonald (NYPD officer)

**KEY FINDING:** Craig Hospital is the most significant TBI rehabilitation facility serving Wyoming patients. Wyoming has no equivalent facility. Wyoming patients with serious TBI would likely be transferred to Craig Hospital.

**Source:** Wikipedia article on Craig Hospital (fetched June 2026)

## 4. LEGAL PATHWAY FOR INMATE WITH BRAIN INJURY IN WYOMING

### 4A. Wyoming Department of Corrections (WDOC) Overview

- **Headquarters:** Suite 100, 1934 Wyott Drive, Cheyenne, WY
- **Established:** 1991 (reorganized from Wyoming State Board of Charities and Reform)
- **Director:** Bob Lampert (as of 2019 reporting)
- **Facilities (5 total):**
  1. Wyoming State Penitentiary — Rawlins (maximum security)
  2. Wyoming Women's Center — Lusk (capacity 261)
  3. Wyoming Honor Farm — Riverton (capacity 283)
  4. Wyoming Honor Conservation Camp & Boot Camp — Newcastle (capacity 261)
  5. Wyoming Medium Correctional Institution — Torrington (intake center for non-death-sentenced men, opened 2010)
- **Additional:** 3 Adult Community Corrections centers (Casper Reentry Center, Cheyenne Transitional Center, Volunteers of America - Booth Hall), 23 field offices for probation/parole
- **System is overcrowded:** Has leased prison beds from a private facility in Mississippi since 2019
- **Staff turnover:** ~23% average annual turnover
- **Budget cuts:** 180 staff positions cut since 2011

### 4B. WDOC Use of Force Policy

- **No publicly available use of force policy was found** through web research
- WDOC website (corrections.wyo.gov) does not publish policies online
- WDOC states commitment to "reasonable, safe, secure, and humane management of inmates"
- WDOC is subject to American Correctional Association standards
- The WDOC non-discrimination policy covers race, color, national origin, sex, disability, and age
- Grievance process: Facility Grievance Manager for inmates; Facility/Office HR Representative for staff; WDOC Public Information Officer for public

### 4C. WDOC Mental Health & Medical Services

- WDOC assesses risks and needs to target treatment and intervention efforts
- Wyoming State Hospital (Evanston) is the state psychiatric hospital but does NOT have a TBI program
- Inmates with brain injuries would require:
  1. Initial assessment at intake facility (WMCI Torrington for men)
  2. Medical care through WDOC contract medical providers
  3. Potential transfer to outside medical facility for specialized care (CRMC, Craig Hospital)
  4. Psychiatric care through WDOC behavioral health services or Wyoming State Hospital for comorbidities

### 4D. Documented Constitutional Violations in WDOC

**Ellis v. WDOC (2018, U.S. District Court of Wyoming):**
- Three inmates at Wyoming Women's Center filed federal complaint alleging unconstitutional conditions
- Overcrowding, deteriorating facilities, staff shortages, and rising violence
- Women inmates housed in county jails and men's prison due to capacity issues
- Inmate Sheryl Ellis placed in "suicide pod" with video surveillance by male deputies after complaining about conditions
- Wardens allegedly used threats of transfer to county jail as punishment for complaints/grievances
- Women forced into discriminatory practices at county jails (less counseling, education, religious services)
- **Outcome:** Case was filed in forma pauperis in November 2018, pending as of last reporting

**System-wide issues identified:**
- Overcrowding across all facilities
- Staff turnover (8.6% to 33.33% at WWC alone)
- 180 positions eliminated/defunded since 2011
- Budget cuts under Gov. Matt Mead (2016) eliminated 40 correctional officer positions + defunded 122 others
- Inmates shipped to county jails and Mississippi private prison

**Source:** WyoFile investigation (wyofile.com), "Court filing alleges 'unconstitutional' conditions at women's prison"

## 5. WYOMING STATUTES & LEGAL PROTECTIONS FOR INMATES

### 5A. Wyoming Statutes on Excessive Force

- **Wyoming Statutes Title 6** covers Crimes and Offenses, but no specific statute on excessive force by law enforcement was directly accessible
- Wyoming has no standalone statute explicitly protecting inmates from excessive force beyond federal constitutional protections
- **Federal protections apply:** Eighth Amendment (cruel and unusual punishment), Fourteenth Amendment (due process)
- **42 U.S.C. § 1983** (civil rights lawsuits) is the primary legal vehicle for inmates to sue for excessive force
- Wyoming state tort claims act provides limited remedies

### 5B. Wyoming Repeal Gun Free Zones Act (2025)

- Effective July 1, 2025: Allows concealed carry in many state-owned buildings
- **WDOC specifically exempted:** Wyoming Statutes 6-8-104(t) and 6-8-105(d)(v) exempt WDOC employees, contractors, and visitors
- 6-8-105(d)(iv) and 6-5-209 prohibit carrying firearms into penal institutions

### 5C. Inmate Grievance Process

- WDOC has designated Facility Grievance Managers for inmates
- Non-discrimination policy enforced through WDOC
- Inmates can file federal civil rights lawsuits (as demonstrated by Ellis case)

## 6. DOJ PATTERN-OR-PRACTICE INVESTIGATIONS IN WYOMING

### 6A. Findings

**No DOJ pattern-or-practice investigation of any Wyoming law enforcement agency or correctional facility has been identified** through available public sources.

- The DOJ Civil Rights Division Special Litigation Section investigates police departments and correctional facilities under 42 U.S.C. § 14141 for patterns of constitutional violations
- No Wyoming cases appeared in public records or news searches
- This is consistent with Wyoming's small population (~580,000) and rural character — DOJ pattern-or-practice investigations have historically focused on larger departments (Chicago PD, Baltimore PD, Ferguson PD, etc.)
- The absence of a DOJ investigation does NOT mean no constitutional violations exist — it means no federal investigation has been initiated
- The Ellis v. WDOC case (2018) and Wyoming's prison overcrowding crisis suggest systemic issues that could warrant federal scrutiny

### 6B. Relevant Federal Oversight Mechanisms

- **DOJ Civil Rights Division** — can investigate under 42 U.S.C. § 14141
- **PREA (Prison Rape Elimination Act)** audits apply to WDOC facilities
- **American Correctional Association (ACA) standards** — WDOC is subject to ACA standards
- **Wyoming ACLU** — has been active on prison conditions issues

## 8. ASSESSMENT: WHERE WOULD A POLICE-BRUTALITY TBI PATIENT BE TREATED IN WYOMING?

### 8A. Likely Treatment Pathway

1. **Acute Trauma Stabilization:**
   - If in Casper area: Banner Wyoming Medical Center (Level II Trauma Center, 249 beds)
   - If in Cheyenne area: Cheyenne Regional Medical Center (Level III Trauma Center with neurosurgery)
   - If elsewhere: Nearest regional hospital, then transfer to Level II/III trauma center

2. **Post-Acute Brain Injury Rehabilitation:**
   - **In-state option:** Cheyenne Regional Medical Center Acute Rehabilitation Unit (19 beds, explicitly treats TBI)
   - **Out-of-state option (most likely for severe TBI):** Craig Hospital, Englewood, CO (93 beds, world-class TBI specialty hospital, accepts out-of-state patients including Wyoming)
   - **Other possibilities:** University of Utah Health Rehabilitation Center (Salt Lake City), Spalding Rehabilitation Hospital (Aurora, CO)

3. **Long-term/Outpatient:**
   - CRMC outpatient therapy services (PT, OT, speech/language)
   - Ivinson Memorial Hospital (Laramie) — has neurology and PT providers
   - Community-based services (very limited in Wyoming)
   - No identified TBI-specific Medicaid waiver in Wyoming

### 8B. Critical Gaps in Wyoming's TBI Infrastructure

1. **No freestanding TBI rehabilitation hospital** in the entire state
2. **Only one inpatient rehab unit** (CRMC ARU, 19 beds) explicitly treats TBI
3. **No identified state brain injury program** or TBI-specific Medicaid waiver
4. **No Brain Injury Association chapter** in Wyoming for resource navigation
5. **Limited neurology/neurosurgery** outside of Cheyenne and Casper
6. **WDOC medical services** for inmates with TBI are unclear and likely inadequate
7. **No DOJ oversight** of Wyoming law enforcement or corrections for pattern-or-practice issues
8. **Documented unconstitutional conditions** in Wyoming prisons (Ellis case)
