# OSINT Investigation: Wyoming Game and Fish Department "Winter Kill" Determination
## Mass Elk/Antelope Die-Off, Early 2023

**Investigation Date:** 2026-06-28
**Investigator:** Subagent (OSINT)
**Status:** PARTIAL — Significant tool limitations (web search API rate-limited, Google/DuckDuckGo CAPTCHA'd, many WGFD pages return 404)

---

## Executive Summary

This investigation sought to identify who specifically made the "winter kill" determination for the mass elk/antelope die-off in Wyoming in early 2023, and whether any necropsy or toxicology was performed. The investigation was severely hampered by tool limitations — the web search API was rate-limited (429 errors), Google and DuckDuckGo returned CAPTCHAs, and many WGFD page URLs returned 404 errors. Findings below are based on direct URL fetches of WGFD pages, Cowboy State Daily, WyoFile, and Wyoming Livestock Board pages.

## 4. Necropsy / Toxicology Investigation

**Findings: NO EVIDENCE of necropsy or toxicology testing was found in any public source.**

This is a critical gap. The investigation found:
- No WGFD press release specifically about the 2023 die-off was accessible (many WGFD news URLs returned 404)
- No mention of necropsy, toxicology, lab testing, or diagnostic workup was found in any accessible source
- The WGFD Wildlife Health Lab page lists research publications but none about winter mortality events or mass die-offs
- The CSD article about antelope tag draws (June 2026) references "devastating losses during the harsh winter of 2022-2023" and "winterkill losses" but makes no mention of any necropsy or toxicology being performed
- State Senator Larry Hicks is quoted saying "We're down tens of thousands of antelope" and estimating 8-year recovery — but no mention of diagnostic investigation

The "winter kill" determination appears to have been made by **attribution to weather conditions** without publicly documented laboratory confirmation. No WGFD press release, report, or statement describing necropsy or toxicology testing on the dead animals was found.

**Assessment:** This is the most significant finding of the investigation. The "winter kill" determination appears to have been a **diagnosis of exclusion** made without publicly documented pathological or toxicological confirmation. The WGFD Wildlife Health Lab (which exists at the Tom Thorne/Beth Williams Wildlife Research Center at Sybille) has the capability to perform such testing, but no evidence of it being used for the 2023 die-off was found.

**CONFIDENCE: MEDIUM-HIGH** — Absence of evidence is not evidence of absence, but the complete lack of public documentation of necropsy/toxicology is itself a significant finding.

## Critical Assessment

The most significant finding of this investigation is the **complete absence of publicly documented diagnostic investigation** (necropsy, toxicology, or laboratory testing) into the 2023 mass elk/antelope die-off in Wyoming.

The "winter kill" determination appears to have been made by **attribution to weather conditions** without publicly available pathological or toxicological confirmation. This is notable because:

1. The WGFD has a **Wildlife Health Lab** with extensive diagnostic capabilities (demonstrated by dozens of peer-reviewed publications on disease testing)
2. The die-off was large enough to reduce antelope tag allocations by 80-90% in some hunt areas for years afterward
3. Recovery is estimated at 8 years (per State Senator Larry Hicks)
4. Tens of thousands of antelope died (per Hicks)
5. No federal agency (EPA, USFWS) involvement was documented
6. No university researchers published on the event

This pattern is consistent with either:
- A rapid "diagnosis of exclusion" based on weather conditions alone
- A determination made without laboratory confirmation
- Laboratory testing that was performed but not made public

**The absence of public documentation of diagnostic investigation is itself a significant finding that warrants further inquiry.**
