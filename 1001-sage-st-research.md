#### Key Historical Facts About Evanston:

- **Founded:** November 1868 as a Union Pacific Railroad division point
- **Location:** Uinta County, Wyoming. Coordinates: 41°16'5.81"N 110°57'47.65"W. Elevation: 6,749 ft
- **ZIP Code:** 82930
- **Population (2020):** 11,747
- **Streets oriented to railroad tracks**, not compass points — all streets run NE-SW and NW-SE
- **Downtown Evanston Historic District:** Bounded by Center, 9th, 11th, and Front Sts. ~60 buildings, 17 acres. Listed on NRHP November 25, 1983. Built 1880-1930.
- **1001 Sage Street** would be at approximately 10th & Sage — **within or immediately adjacent to the Downtown Evanston Historic District boundary** (bounded by 9th to 11th streets)

> "By the mid-1980s, as the boom began to recede, many of the established businesses downtown closed their doors, including **Blyth & Fargo, which had operated for 107 years**."

**Key facts:**

**Key facts:**

- The hospital was established 1887, opened 1889
- Located on a hill about **one mile south of downtown** Evanston
- The tunnels were for transport of meals, supplies, and patients

#### Wyoming State Hospital vs. Tuberculosis Hospital

- No evidence found of a tuberculosis hospital/sanatorium specifically in Evanston
- The Wyoming State Hospital was always psychiatric (insane asylum → state hospital)
- Wyoming's high altitude (6,749 ft) and dry climate were considered therapeutic for TB, and many Western towns had TB sanatoriums, but Evanston's main medical institution was the psychiatric hospital
- **Possible conflation:** Steven may be conflating the Wyoming State Hospital (psychiatric, with tunnels) with a TB hospital. Or there may have been a TB ward/clinic that operated in a downtown building that is not well-documented online

**100+ immigrants were real** — Chinese immigrants in Evanston, 1880 census. But they were buried in the city cemetery potter's field, not under any building.

**Tuberculosis hospital is UNVERIFIED** — The State Hospital was psychiatric. However, the high altitude and dry climate of Wyoming attracted TB patients, and it's possible a TB ward operated in a converted building. This claim requires further research with local historical societies.

**The building at 1001 Sage Street exists** — confirmed by Google Maps. Its specific history, construction date, and ownership chain could not be determined through online sources alone. Direct contact with the Uinta County Assessor, Uinta County Museum, and Wyoming State Archives is needed.

- Front Street = the street closest to the railroad tracks

> "the administrative wing has a full basement with a connection to the **underground campus corridor system**, connecting to the Women's Building through the north wing and to Whalen Hall through the east wing"

From the WyoHistory article (verbatim):
> "tunnels were built linking the buildings' basements. The tunnels allowed for easier transport of meals and supplies in winter and secure transport of patients year round."

The tunnel system at the State Hospital is well-documented. The State Hospital is located on a hill about one mile south of downtown Evanston. Whether any tunnel system extends to downtown buildings (including 1001 Sage St) is not documented in any source found. However, the distance (approximately one mile) makes it unlikely that the State Hospital tunnels connect to a downtown building. It is more likely that a building at 1001 Sage St might have had its own basement/cellar system, or that the tunnel claim is a transposition of the State Hospital's real tunnels.
