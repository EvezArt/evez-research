#### 1. Eigencartogrophonology
- **Roots:** German *eigen* (own, intrinsic) + Greek *carto* (map) + Greek *graph* (write) + Greek *-ology* (study of)

- **Assessment:** Etymologically valid construction. The consonant cluster "phono" (sound) vs "phono" (as in phonology) creates ambiguity. Intended as "mapping the intrinsic structure of a field."

#### 2. Neuralography
- **Roots:** Latin *neura* (nerve) + Greek *graphia* (writing, description)
- **Meaning:** The written description of neural systems; neural mapping
- **Assessment:** Etymologically sound. Similar to "neurography" but with the adjectival form preserved. Could be confused with neuroimaging but functions as a broader epistemological term.

#### 3. Interventionalmatonomies
- **Roots:** Latin *intervention* (coming between) + Greek *mata* (things, processes) + Greek *-nomy* (law, system)
- **Meaning:** The study of systems that intervene in other systems; the laws governing interventions
- **Assessment:** Unusual construction — the "-mata" infix is Greek but creates a non-standard compound. The intent is clear: the study of interventionary systems. Not a standard academic construction but etymologically traceable.

#### 4. Interspectraloptimetrics
- **Roots:** Latin *inter* (between) + Latin *spectrum* (appearance, image) + Latin *optim* (best) + Greek *-metrics* (measurement)
- **Meaning:** Measuring optimal interactions between spectra (of evidence, of systems)
- **Assessment:** Compound is dense but each root is traceable. Functions as "the measurement of cross-spectral optimization." Valid as a constructed technical term.

- **Roots:** Greek *onta* (beings, existing things) + Greek *tax* (arrangement) + Greek *-nomy* (law) + Greek *-graph* (write) + Greek *-etics* (study of)

- **Assessment:** Extremely dense compound — 5 morphemes in one word. Each root is valid. The word is structurally sound but practically unwieldy. Functions as "the writing of the laws of the arrangement of existing things."

#### 6. Autographenlemnics
