# OSINT Investigation: Federal Civil Rights Lawsuits in the U.S. District Court for the District of Wyoming

**Investigation Date:** 2026-06-28
**Investigator:** EVEZ OSINT Subagent
**Scope:** Unpublished federal civil rights lawsuits involving brain injury, excessive force, or police brutality in the District of Wyoming — cases that would NOT appear in appellate court records.

---

## Executive Summary

This investigation identified **multiple active and recent federal civil rights lawsuits** filed in the U.S. District Court for the District of Wyoming involving excessive force, police brutality, brain injury, and civil rights violations. Wyoming has a notably low rate of civil rights litigation against law enforcement (only 2 constitutional claims in 2024, 8 in 2023, 13 at peak in 2020), but several significant cases are active in federal district court. The total known payout for Wyoming police settlements between FY2019–FY2023 was nearly **$3.4 million** across 86 settlements.

**CRITICAL FINDING:** A brain injury civil rights lawsuit has been filed in the District of Wyoming — the **Benjamin Burks v. Park County Sheriff's Office** case, involving a "significant brain injury and facial disfiguration" from excessive force by deputies in the Park County Detention Center.

### CASE 2: Powell v. City of Lander, Wells, Tadewald (EXCESSIVE FORCE / PEPPER SPRAY)

- **Plaintiff:** Kayvon Powell (Black man, moved from Charlottesville, VA)
- **Defendants:** City of Lander, Lander Police Department, Sgt. Ron Wells, Officer Casey Tadewald
- **Court:** U.S. District Court, District of Wyoming (implied federal civil rights filing)
- **Filed:** September 2, 2025 (Tuesday before Sept 4, 2025 article)
- **Nature:** 42 U.S.C. § 1983 — First and Fourth Amendment violations, excessive force
- **Attorneys:** Spence Law Firm (Jackson, WY) — Noah Drew; Bailey Lazzari (Lander)
- **Source:** WyoFile, September 4, 2025

**Details:**
- Sept 3, 2024 arrest: Sgt. Ron Wells stopped Powell for alleged reckless driving (charge later dropped before trial).
- Wells opened Powell's car door without permission; Powell had a broken window.
- Officers removed Powell from car, handcuffed him, placed him in police cruiser.
- Officer Tadewald then blasted the handcuffed Powell with pepper spray in the back seat.
- Wells kept rear windows closed as Powell pleaded for air, telling him "that's what you get."
- Fremont County judge described Wells as "out of line, absolutely 100% out of line" during Powell's trial.
- Powell was convicted of "interfering with a police officer" (misdemeanor), sentenced to probation with suspended 45-day jail. He is appealing.
- Lawsuit alleges "gratuitous and excessive force" and unconstitutional department pepper spray policy.
- Powell did not allege racial discrimination in the suit but told media he believes appearance impacted treatment.
- Interim Police Chief Kelly Waugh acknowledged Wells' actions were "very unprofessional" in a recorded conversation.
- City of Lander has neither apologized nor made amends.

**Status:** Active/pending. Filed September 2025.

### CASE 3: Laramore Family v. Thermopolis Police Department / Mascorro (WRONGFUL DEATH / ILLEGAL ENTRY)

- **Plaintiffs:** Buck Laramore's mother and widow (Brandi Laramore)
- **Defendants:** Thermopolis Police Department, Sgt. Michael Mascorro
- **Court:** U.S. District Court of Wyoming
- **Filed:** ~2024 (article published by WyoFile; lawsuit filed "this week" per article)
- **Nature:** Wrongful death, Fourth Amendment violation (illegal home entry), 42 U.S.C. § 1983
- **Source:** WyoFile

**Details:**
- April 28, 2023: Sgt. Michael Mascorro (2nd in command at TPD, 8-year veteran, former USAF) entered Laramore's trailer home WITHOUT a warrant.
- Laramore had been investigated for meth use at McDonald's where he worked. Mascorro wanted to arrest him for interference (giving false name/birth year — misdemeanor).
- Mascorro shouldered through the door after Laramore refused to come outside.
- Laramore shot Mascorro first (.45-caliber through arm into chest, broke rib, through lung, near spine).
- Mascorro then shot Laramore 7 times, then 3 more times. Laramore died in his wife's arms.
- Sweetwater County DA Dan Erramouspe cleared Mascorro of homicide charges but found he **entered the home illegally**.
- POST nearly decertified Mascorro but did not. Town officials allowed him to return to duty in June 2024.
- Mascorro had **4 prior use-of-force complaints** including kicking a man and escalating an encounter at a bar.
- Lawsuit alleges Mascorro's aggressive behavior should have been corrected and the shooting was preventable.
- Cites Wyoming "stand your ground" and castle doctrine — Laramore was defending against a lawless intruder.

**Status:** Active/pending in federal court.

**Source URLs:**
- https://wyofile.com/wrongful-death-suit-challenges-thermopolis-cop-for-illegally-entering-home/

### CASE 4: Eckerson v. City of Casper, Ramsay, Burns (EXCESSIVE FORCE / ILLEGAL HOME ENTRY)

- **Plaintiff:** Brenton Eckerson
- **Defendants:** City of Casper, Officer Jon Ramsay, Officer Craig Burns
- **Court:** U.S. District Court, District of Wyoming
- **Filed:** ~October 2024 (article published shortly after Oct 10, 2024)
- **Nature:** 42 U.S.C. § 1983 — First and Fourth Amendment violations, excessive force, illegal entry
- **Attorney:** Darold Killmer (Killmer Lane)
- **Source:** WyoFile

**Details:**
- March 5, 2022: Officers responded to calls about Eckerson pushing over a birdbath and flipping off cars.
- Ramsay drew his gun immediately on the intoxicated 39-year-old in his own yard.
- Eckerson retreated into his home. Officers entered WITHOUT a warrant and no exigent circumstances.
- Ramsay pushed Eckerson into a banister (breaking the railing). Burns hit Eckerson in face and body.
- Eckerson's **ACL was torn**. His blood was all over his face, floor, and Burns' hands.
- Burns told a commanding officer he threw "a couple strikes," busting "up his face a little."
- Lawsuit alleges inadequate training by Casper PD, citing Sgt. Jake Bigelow's testimony that Wyoming officers get no Fourth Amendment training beyond the academy.
- Pattern allegation: multiple prior Casper PD settlements cited.

**Status:** Active/pending. Responses from defendants expected.

**Source URLs:**
- https://wyofile.com/inadequately-trained-casper-police-violated-a-mans-rights-lawsuit-alleges/

- **Source:** WyoFile

**Details:**
- Jacobs serving 25-35 year sentence at Wyoming Women's Center (WWC) in Lusk since 2020 (arrested at 19).
- July 2023: Gaul raped Jacobs in a chemical closet, then assaulted her again ~1 week later.

- Gaul was fired from at least 2 Nebraska public-sector jobs for misconduct toward women BEFORE being hired at WWC in 2019.

- Warden Lang and Deputy Warden Hardy accused of deliberate indifference.
- Wyoming AG filed motion to dismiss (Nov 2025) claiming 11th Amendment immunity, statute of limitations, and failure to state a claim.

## 2. Recently Resolved Federal Civil Rights Cases (Settled/Judged)

### Rodriguez v. Quirin / City of Casper (JUDGMENT — $155,000)

- **Plaintiff:** Adrianah "Nana" Rodriguez (Latina/Black woman, 120 lbs, 5'3")
- **Defendant:** Officer Michael Quirin, City of Casper
- **Court:** U.S. District Court, District of Wyoming
- **Resolved:** October 2024
- **Outcome:** $130,000 judgment against Quirin + $25,000 settlement with city (paid by WARM municipal liability pool)
- **Nature:** Fourth Amendment violation, excessive force, First Amendment retaliation
- **Attorney:** Darold Killmer (Killmer Lane)

**Details:**
- January 5, 2020: Quirin responded to noise complaint at Casper apartment.
- Bodycam recorded him saying "It's not that loud" before entering apartment on marijuana scent pretext.
- Quirin slammed Rodriguez to ground, kneeled on her neck (5 months before George Floyd).
- Rodriguez charged with interference; charges later dropped.
- Quirin suspended 1 week without pay, required additional training.
- Quirin offered to have judgment entered against him (unusual) — suggests recognition of indefensibility.
- Quirin remains a patrol officer at Casper PD. Chief said he "does fine work."
- **Prior Casper settlements cited:** $149,000 (dragging man from truck), $85,000 (unwarranted home entry), $200,000 (gunpoint detention of mother and child).

### Ramirez Family v. Albany County / Colling (SETTLED — $1.2 MILLION)

- **Plaintiff:** Deb Hinkel (mother of Robbie Ramirez)
- **Defendant:** Albany County, Deputy Derek Colling
- **Court:** U.S. District Court, District of Wyoming (settled before trial)
- **Outcome:** $1.2 million settlement
- **Nature:** Wrongful death, Fourth Amendment violation, excessive force
- **Source:** WyoFile

**Details:**
- 2018: Robbie Ramirez, unarmed and mentally ill, stopped by Deputy Derek Colling for minor traffic violation.
- Colling shot Ramirez twice in the back.
- Colling had previously been cleared of 2 fatal shootings as a Las Vegas police officer.
- Hinkel alleged Albany County altered Colling's bodycam footage to delete damning scenes (denied by county).
- Federal judge found "no legal basis for evidence tampering" — but arguments about footage deletion never fully heard.
- Settlement included "soft commitment" to continue mental health training for deputies.
- POST decertification of Colling delayed multiple times; Albany County refused to release personnel records even after legislative change.
- Settlement was initially confidential, released after threat of lawsuit by media.

## 3. Jail Death Cases (Potential/Pending Federal Litigation)

### Campbell County Detention Center — Kenneth Durrah Death (2022)

- **Deceased:** Kenneth R. Durrah (5'6", 142 lbs)
- **Date of Death:** December 1, 2022
- **Location:** Campbell County Detention Center, Gillette, WY
- **Circumstances:** Pinned face-down for ~5 minutes by 4 guards (one on head, one on back, two on legs). Cut jumpsuit off, strapped naked to restraint chair. Complained "I can't breathe" and "I'm dying, help." Found unresponsive 35-40 minutes later. Pronounced dead at hospital.
- **Prosecutor Decision:** Weston County Attorney Michael Stulken (special prosecutor) declined criminal charges (March 2024).
- **Autopsy:** Forensic pathologist Thomas Bennett ruled death methamphetamine overdose, not "restraint related." However, Bennett was NOT provided video before making determination and has faced scrutiny in Iowa/Montana for prior work.
- **Expert Review:** Multiple experts (Eric Jaeger, Dr. Brooks Walsh, retired sheriff Gary Raney) told WyoFile that 5+ minutes prone with weight on back likely contributed to death. DOJ has warned since 1995 that belly-down restraints can be lethal.
- **No policy changes** made by Campbell County Sheriff's Office.
- **Lawsuit Status:** **No lawsuit filed as of latest reporting.** Family member Joshua Durrah expressed frustration. This is a strong candidate for a future federal civil rights lawsuit.
- **Sheriff:** Scott Matheny stands by jail staff. Jail Captain Kevin Theis acknowledged "we probably should have" called EMS sooner.

## 12. Limitations and Recommendations for Further Investigation

### Limitations

1. **PACER Access:** Direct PACER searches of the District of Wyoming docket were not possible. A PACER account and case search would reveal all filings, including sealed cases and pro se filings not covered by news media.
2. **RECAP Coverage:** CourtListener's RECAP archive has limited coverage for District of Wyoming due to low user contributions.
3. **Paywalled Sources:** Several key articles (Casper Star-Tribune, Wyoming Tribune Eagle, Cody Enterprise full article) are behind paywalls, limiting detail extraction.
4. **Sealed/Confidential Settlements:** Wyoming has a history of confidential police settlements (e.g., Ramirez settlement was initially secret). The Wyoming Supreme Court has ruled that confidentiality agreements in government settlements violate state law, but compliance is ongoing.
5. **Pro Se Filings:** Inmate pro se filings in District of Wyoming may not be covered by news media or RECAP.

### Recommendations

1. **PACER Search:** Search the District of Wyoming PACER system directly for:
   - Nature of suit: 440 (Other Civil Rights) and 550 (Prisoner Prisoner Condition)
   - Filed date range: 2023-2026
   - Cause: 42 U.S.C. § 1983
   - Keywords: "brain injury," "excessive force"

2. **Burks Case Monitoring:** Monitor the Burks v. Park County case for filed pleadings, especially the complaint (for injury details and named deputies) and any motion to dismiss.

3. **Durrah Family:** Contact the Durrah family or their attorney to determine if a federal civil rights lawsuit is being prepared for the Campbell County jail death.

4. **Wyoming P&A:** Contact Wyoming Protection and Advocacy (wypanda.com) directly to inquire about any brain injury cases involving law enforcement they may be investigating or litigating.

5. **Federal Court Records:** Monitor the District of Wyoming for any new § 1983 filings in 2026, particularly from inmates at county detention centers.
