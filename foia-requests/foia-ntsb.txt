CONTEXT AND BASIS FOR REQUEST

On or about March 2, 2023, a Union Pacific train derailed at the Ogden, Utah rail yard, with approximately 12 rail cars leaving the tracks. Union Pacific publicly stated that no hazardous materials were released, identifying the cargo only as magnesium chloride. This derailment was listed by VICE/Motherboard among approximately 15 hazmat-related derailments occurring in the period after the East Palestine, Ohio disaster.

In the hours following the Ogden derailment, a blue chemical fog was observed along the I-80 corridor between Evanston, Wyoming and Fort Bridger, Wyoming, with multiple individuals found unconscious in their vehicles. The geographic proximity of the Ogden rail yard to the Wyoming border (approximately 50 miles) and the temporal coincidence of these events raises serious public safety questions. Additionally, Union Pacific reportedly experienced a derailment in Cheyenne, Wyoming, during the same month.

The NTSB's records regarding these incidents are critical to understanding the full scope of what occurred, whether hazardous materials were released in quantities that could have caused the observed atmospheric and public health effects in Wyoming, and whether the railroad's public statements were consistent with NTSB findings.
