Subject: FOIA / Wyoming Public Records Act Request — Chemical Spill Reports, Release Notifications, and Incident Reports for the I-80 Corridor and Union Pacific Rail Corridor in Uinta County, Wyoming (February 1, 2023 – April 30, 2023)

Pursuant to the Wyoming Public Records Act (Wyo. Stat. § 16-4-201 et seq.) and applicable federal Freedom of Information Act provisions, I hereby request the following public records from the Wyoming Department of Environmental Quality (DEQ):

SCOPE OF REQUEST

All spill reports, incident reports, chemical release notifications, environmental complaint records, emergency response records, and any related correspondence, inspection reports, or enforcement documents pertaining to the Interstate 80 (I-80) corridor and the Union Pacific Railroad corridor within Uinta County, Wyoming, for the period of February 1, 2023 through April 30, 2023.

SPECIFIC RECORDS REQUESTED

1. All spill or release notifications received by Wyoming DEQ for the I-80 corridor in Uinta County, Wyoming, between February 1, 2023 and April 30, 2023, including but not limited to:
   - Any notifications involving magnesium chloride, cyclohexane, ammonium nitrate, or any other hazardous material or regulated substance;

2. All incident reports, complaint reports, or investigation files generated by or referred to Wyoming DEQ concerning Union Pacific Railroad operations in Uinta County, Wyoming, during the stated time period, including but not limited to:
   - Reports of chemical fog, vapor clouds, unusual odors, or atmospheric releases along the I-80 corridor;
   - Reports of vehicles, motorists, or residents affected by airborne chemical exposure along I-80 between Evanston, Wyoming and Fort Bridger, Wyoming;
   - Reports of unconscious individuals or medical emergencies linked to suspected chemical exposure along the I-80 corridor;

3. Any correspondence, memoranda, emails, or communications between Wyoming DEQ and any of the following entities regarding incidents along the I-80 corridor or Union Pacific rail corridor in Uinta County during the stated period:
   - Union Pacific Railroad;
   - Uinta County Emergency Management;
   - Wyoming Highway Patrol;
   - Wyoming Office of Homeland Security / Emergency Management;
   - U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) Region 8;
   - National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB);
   - Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety Administration (PHMSA);
   - Any local fire department, HAZMAT team, or emergency medical services;

4. All Wyoming DEQ inspection reports, site assessment reports, soil/water/air sampling results, or environmental monitoring records related to any actual or suspected chemical release along the I-80 corridor or Union Pacific rail corridor in Uinta County, Wyoming, between February 1, 2023 and April 30, 2023;

5. Any records referencing, relating to, or arising from the Union Pacific train derailment at the Ogden, Utah rail yard on or about March 2, 2023, insofar as those records pertain to environmental impacts, chemical migration, or release events affecting Uinta County, Wyoming;

6. Any records pertaining to magnesium chloride transport by rail through Uinta County, Wyoming, including any reported releases, leaks, or spills of magnesium chloride during the stated period.

CONTEXT AND BASIS FOR REQUEST

On or about March 2, 2023, a Union Pacific train derailed at the Ogden, Utah rail yard, resulting in approximately 12 rail cars leaving the tracks. Union Pacific publicly stated that no hazardous materials were released and that the only cargo of concern was magnesium chloride. Reports from VICE/Motherboard listed this derailment among approximately 15 hazardous materials derailments occurring in the period following the East Palestine, Ohio derailment.

In the hours following the Ogden derailment, a chemical fog was observed along the I-80 corridor between Evanston, Wyoming and Fort Bridger, Wyoming, in Uinta County. Multiple individuals were reported unconscious in vehicles along the interstate. The proximity in time and geography between the Ogden derailment and the Wyoming chemical fog event raises significant public health and environmental concerns that warrant disclosure of all responsive records.

ADDITIONAL CONSIDERATIONS

This request is submitted in the public interest. The information sought relates to potential public health hazards, environmental contamination, and public safety along a major interstate corridor used by the general public. The requested records are expected to shed light on the adequacy of chemical release reporting, emergency response, and environmental monitoring along the I-80 corridor in Uinta County, Wyoming.

I request a fee waiver for all search, review, and duplication costs on the basis that this request is not made for commercial purposes and the disclosure of the requested information is in the public interest because it is likely to contribute significantly to public understanding of government operations and activities, particularly regarding environmental protection, public safety, and emergency response along a heavily traveled interstate corridor. If any fees cannot be waived, please notify me in advance before processing the request if costs are expected to exceed $50.00.
