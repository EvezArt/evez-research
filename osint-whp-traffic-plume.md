## Executive Summary

This investigation examines the Wyoming Highway Patrol's (WHP) role in directing traffic through a chemical plume on I-80 in Wyoming (mile markers 15–33) during late February/early March 2023, and identifies the chain of command that authorized this action. The investigation confirms the WHP organizational structure, chain of command, the I-80 closure timeline, and the relevant authorities. Several search tools were rate-limited during this investigation, limiting completeness on misconduct lawsuits and Union Pacific connections.

### Geographic Context: Mile Markers 15–33

According to Wikipedia's detailed article on Interstate 80 in Wyoming:
- **Mile markers 6–28** on I-80 (between Evanston and Fort Bridger) is known as the **"Three Sisters"** area, sometimes called the *"Highway to Heaven"* due to an optical illusion.
- This stretch runs through **Uinta County** (Evanston area) and into **Sweetwater County** (toward Green River).
- I-80 **parallels the Union Pacific Railroad** throughout this entire section — the Evanston Subdivision rail line.
- The reported mile markers 15–33 overlap substantially with the "Three Sisters" area and extend into Sweetwater County.

**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstate_80_in_Wyoming

### Analysis: Official vs. Reported Narrative

The official WYDOT narrative attributes the I-80 closure (Feb 18–28, 2023) entirely to **winter weather / snow drifts**. However:
- Steven Crawford-Maggard reports that law enforcement (WHP) directed traffic **through a chemical plume** rather than around it during this period.
- The closure period (10 days) is unusually long even for Wyoming winter storms.
- The official narrative makes **no mention of any chemical event, hazmat response, or air quality concerns**.
- No evacuation orders or air quality testing has been documented in public sources.
- The presence of **people unconscious in vehicles** in the plume zone (as reported by Steven) is absent from all official communications.

This discrepancy suggests either:
1. The chemical event was not officially recognized or documented, OR
2. The chemical event was documented but records have not been made public, OR
3. The chemical event was conflated with or masked by the winter storm narrative.

## 3. WHP Authority vs. Other Agencies

### WHP Authority
- WHP is a division of **WYDOT** (Wyoming Department of Transportation)
- WHP Troopers have full law enforcement authority statewide
- WHP operates a **centralized dispatch center** in Cheyenne that coordinates troopers statewide
- WHP dispatch also serves: Wyoming DCI, Wyoming Game and Fish, Wyoming Outfitter Board, Wyoming Livestock Agents, BLM Law Enforcement, US Forest Service Rangers, State Brand Inspectors, State Park Rangers (via SALECS radio system)

**Source:** https://whp.wyo.gov/dispatch

### WYDOT Authority
- WYDOT is responsible for maintaining and operating state highways, including I-80
- WYDOT's **Snowplow Priority Plan** designates I-80 as a "24-hour road"
- WYDOT compiles road closure data
- **Both WHP and WYDOT maintenance personnel can initiate road closures** — the WYDOT closure data page states: *"Data reflects full closures initiated by maintenance employees or Highway Patrol Troopers."*

**Source:** https://dot.state.wy.us/data

### Who Decides I-80 Closures?
Based on the evidence:
1. **WHP Troopers** can initiate closures on-scene
2. **WYDOT maintenance employees** can also initiate closures
3. The decision to **reopen** appears to be a joint WHP/WYDOT decision
4. **WHP dispatch** in Cheyenne coordinates the response
5. Ultimate authority rests with the **WHP Colonel** (Cameron) and **WYDOT Director** (Luke Reiner during 2023)

### Wyoming Division of Criminal Investigation (DCI)
- Separate agency under the **Wyoming Attorney General's Office** (not WYDOT)
- DCI investigates at the request of local law enforcement, or at the direction of the Governor
- DCI has field offices in **Evanston, Rock Springs, Rawlins, Laramie** — all along I-80
- DCI handles: drug enforcement, organized crime, domestic terrorism, homeland security disasters
- DCI would be the agency to investigate potential criminal negligence related to the incident

**Source:** https://wyomingdci.wyo.gov/dci-homepage

### Local Sheriffs
- The **Uinta County Sheriff** (Evanston area, mile markers 6–28) and **Sweetwater County Sheriff** (Green River/Rock Springs area, mile markers 28+) would have concurrent jurisdiction
- Local sheriffs can request DCI investigations

### Hazmat Response
- No specific Wyoming hazmat response protocol for I-80 was found in public sources during this investigation (search was rate-limited)
- Wyoming DCI's jurisdiction includes "domestic terrorism or disasters related to homeland security" — this could encompass chemical events
- The absence of any documented hazmat response is a significant finding — if a chemical plume was present, standard emergency response protocols would typically require:
  - EPA notification
  - Wyoming Department of Environmental Quality involvement
  - Local fire department/hazmat team activation
  - Air quality monitoring
  - Evacuation orders for affected areas

## 5. Union Pacific Railroad Connection

### Geographic Context
I-80 **parallels the Union Pacific Railroad** throughout the entire area in question:
- The **Evanston Subdivision** rail line runs alongside I-80 from the Utah state line through Evanston
- Near mile marker 28 (west of Fort Bridger), I-80 crosses over the UP rail line
- East of Rock Springs, I-80 parallels the UP's **Rawlins Subdivision**
- The entire I-80 corridor in Wyoming follows the historic Union Pacific right-of-way

**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstate_80_in_Wyoming

### Potential UP Influence
Search for direct connections between Union Pacific and WHP was **inconclusive** due to rate limiting. However:
- Union Pacific is one of the **largest landowners and economic forces** in Wyoming
- The UP rail line runs immediately adjacent to I-80 through the incident zone
- Any chemical event on I-80 could potentially affect UP operations
- UP has a **vested economic interest** in keeping I-80 open (parallel highway access for trucking competitors, emergency access, employee commuting)
- The question of whether UP requested or pressured WHP to keep I-80 open during a chemical event **cannot be confirmed or denied** from public sources accessed in this investigation
- This would be a key area for further investigation via:
  - Public records requests to WHP for communications with UP during Feb/March 2023
  - Federal Railroad Administration records
  - UP's own incident reports (if any)
